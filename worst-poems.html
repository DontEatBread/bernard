The Ballad of the Missing Sock

My sock is gone, where could it be? It wasn’t on the floor, I see. I checked the drawer, I checked the chair, But there’s no sock, it’s just not there.

I looked inside the laundry bin, No sock, no sock, where have you been? Did you escape? Did you run away? Why won’t you come back to me today?

Oh, sock, you’ve left me all alone, My foot is cold, I’ll never be whole. Now I walk with only one, A barefoot journey has begun.

The Epic of the Apple

An apple fell, it hit the ground, It made a noise, it made a sound. It rolled away, I chased it fast, But I couldn’t catch it, not at last.

I picked it up, it was so red, But then I dropped it, onto the bed. I thought about it, for a while, But now I’m bored, and I don’t smile.

I ate the apple, it was so sweet, But now my stomach has a seat. The apple’s gone, it’s all done now, I wonder what I’ll eat next, anyhow.

The Tale of the Rubber Duck

The rubber duck floats in the tub, With a little squeak, it’s a tiny nub. I watch it float, it’s kind of neat, But then it’s stuck under my feet.

It bounces up, it bounces down, It makes a sound, a little frown. I wish it could just go away, But it’s here with me, every day.

The rubber duck is yellow and round, It doesn’t really make a sound. It’s just there, not much to say, But it keeps me company in a strange way.
